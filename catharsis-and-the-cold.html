Catharsis and the Cold

By Therizinosaurus Cheloniformis
March 6, 2023

When It is cold, and I stand outside - in the blistering cold and light drizzle - for two hours doing nothing, my mind often begins to do one of two things:

  1. Wander
  2. Hyper-focus

When the first happens, I usually think about quite existential topics which I do not really understand. Like death and love, and make up terrible post-modernist ideas in my mind that either don't make much sense, or have already been discovered.

When my mind does the other, it often leads to stress. Huge amounts of stress. I worry about the smallest things, and the things that do not really matter, but are able to fill my mind in those solemn hours of standing in the cold.


The cold seeps into the centres of my limbs, through the soles of my feet, a thick, slow, creeping cold that seeps in and stays there, even when I am inside, in the (legally required) 16℃ warmth, though my fingers may slowly become accustomed to the warmth, my limbs will hide inside them a viscous, deep cold.
