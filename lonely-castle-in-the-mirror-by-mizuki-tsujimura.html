Lonely Castle in the Mirror by Mizuki Tsujimura

By Therizinosaurus Cheloniformis
March 13, 2023

Lonely Castle in the Mirror by Mizuki Tsujimura is a very interesting book. At first, it seems to be quite a childish book, written simply and conveying a story which is almost definitely not something Serious FantasyÂ would ever stoop down to. However, as you delve deeper into the book, you start to see elements which do not fit, and, slowly, you realise that what you are reading is a corrupted fairytale, modernised, mysterious, with dark corners. It is not hard to tell that Tsujimura has also written mysteries, as, at the core of the story is one such mystery.Â 

The characters are all children of varying ages who enter a strange castle through mirrors in their school, called by a strange girl called the wolf queen. Each of the characters is explored, with each of their seemingly bizarre character traits explained through close inspection. The main character, Kokoro is handled especially well, and the empathy with all of the children are depicted with does not let down the social-commentary side of the book (which definitely is not the focus of the book). The ending is by far the best part of the book, a wonderful denouement in which everything is explained, and the emotional punches are packed into an incredibly tense section of the book. The book also manages to pull off a wonderfully complex faux time-travel reveal at the end, all of which comes together to produce a thoroughly enjoyable book.Â
